Scope 1 & 3 ESRS Aligned

Koottam Sustainability Assessment

Sustainability Audit • Energy, Waste & Transport (Scope 1 & 3)

TERP conducted a full-spectrum sustainability audit of the Koottam Festival, analysing energy use (diesel generator performance, load patterns, fuel-to-kWh efficiency), waste flows (organic, recyclables, SL/ML plastics), and transport-related Scope 3 emissions. The assessment followed ESRS-aligned indicators and produced a low-carbon operational roadmap for future editions.

Highlights
  • 3-day field observation covering visitor movement, vendor operations, backstage utilities, and waste stations
  • Comprehensive Scope 1 and Scope 3 emissions analysis
  • Load mapping to identify generator oversizing and idle fuel consumption
  • Clear, implementable recommendations for energy efficiency, better load distribution, and circular waste planning
Mobile First Local Economy

Roots & Routes

Experiential Tourism Mapping • Community & Women-Led Enterprises

Roots & Routes is a digital travel tool designed to shift tourism benefits directly to local households. The platform generates custom travel routes and highlights small entrepreneurs — especially women-led and tribal community businesses — located along those routes. The goal is to promote slow, experiential, low-impact tourism.

Highlights
  • Mobile-first, map-based discovery
  • Integrates local food, craft, cultural, and nature-based experiences
  • Builds economic resilience by spotlighting micro-enterprises
  • Encourages low-carbon travel choices and decentralised tourism
Plastic Flow Avoided Emissions

Clean Kerala Company Assessment

Waste Systems, Plastic Flow Mapping & Avoided Emissions Analysis

TERP assessed CKCL’s waste management system with focus on SL/ML plastics, decentralised recovery potential, and climate impacts of improved collection. We calculated emission factors and avoided CO₂ emissions from waste that would otherwise have been openly burned — a major source of particulate pollution and greenhouse gases.

Key Insights
  • Calculated total waste processed using CKCL’s collection data, following IPCC-compliant methods for waste characterisation and emission pathways
  • Assessed the monetary value generated through waste recovery operations
  • Derived emission factors for open burning using IPCC 2006 / 2019 Refinement guidelines and calculated avoided CO₂ emissions
  • Poster-style impact visualisations for public communication

SDG 4 Tribal Education

Chayakkoodu

Tribal Arts & Environmental Education Residency

Chayakkoodu is a creative learning residency at a tribal LP school in Kannur, combining art, storytelling, and environmental education. Students explored themes of forest identity, local wildlife, and cultural memory, culminating in a collaborative mural created with tribal educators.

Highlights
  • Hands-on art sessions centred on place, ecology, and culture
  • Reinforced SDG 4 by building confidence through creative expression
  • Community mural co-created with students and teachers
  • Encouraged children to interpret their forest environment through colour, symbols, and narrative
